what it does
01
You see what it sees.

Live camera feed streams directly from the ESP32 camera module to your browser. Real-time, in the room, no preprocessing. If something moves, you see it move.

camera stream
02
Your voice, in the room.

Hold to speak and your audio plays through the buddy's speaker. You're not sending a message — you're talking into the room. The people there hear you like you're in it.

two-way audio
03
You move it. Actually move it.

The directional pad sends motor commands over WebSocket to the ESP32. No lag, no buffering — just press a direction and the buddy goes there.

motor control
04
One ID. That's the whole setup.

Your buddy prints an ID on boot. You type that ID here. The connection is established peer-to-peer over your local network. Nothing leaves the house unless you want it to.

local network
first connection
step 01
Flash your buddy and power it on.

Upload the buddy firmware to your ESP32. On boot it connects to your home wifi and starts listening for an incoming WebSocket connection.

step 02
Find the buddy ID.

The ID is printed on the underside of the device. It also appears in the ESP32 serial monitor on first boot. It looks like this:

BDY-00441
step 03
Make sure you're on the same network.

Your computer and the buddy both need to be on the same local wifi network. The hub server runs on your machine — the ESP32 connects directly to it.

ws://[your-ip]:3000/ws
?id=BDY-XXXXX&role=device
step 04
Type the ID and connect.

Enter the five-digit code in the connect panel at the top of this page and hit connect. The hub pairs with your buddy and the camera feed goes live.

note

The hub server must be running before you connect. Start it with node server.js from the project folder. Your local IP (for the ESP32 firmware) can be found with ipconfig on Windows or ifconfig on Mac/Linux.

under the hood

Hardware.
Software.
Nothing else.

Buddy runs entirely on an ESP32 microcontroller. There is no backend, no cloud relay, no subscription. The website connects directly to the device on your LAN.

controller ESP32 — dual-core, 240MHz, 4MB flash
camera OV2640 — up to 1600x1200, JPEG output
audio in INMP441 — I2S MEMS microphone
audio out MAX98357 — I2S 3W amplifier
transport WebSocket — full duplex, no polling
network 802.11 b/g/n — connects to your home wifi
pairing buddy ID — printed on device boot, local only
cloud none
